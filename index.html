Philosophy

Leadership Vision

William Butler Yeats once said, "Education is not the filling of a pail, but the lighting of a fire." Everything I do as a teacher and leader is oriented toward helping students — and institutions — ignite that fire.

I believe a global renaissance is possible if we expand the humanities to embrace Asian traditions alongside Western ones. Globalization dissolves economic borders quickly; but religions, languages, and cultures remain barriers to human understanding. My life's work has been building bridges — between Asian and Abrahamic traditions, between Indian Knowledge Systems and global scholarship, between the insider's reverence and the scholar's rigour.

As a leader, I have learned that institutions succeed when leaders take people with them, listen before they act, and infuse everyday work with energy and soul. I do not lead from a comfort zone — I step outside it, bring conflicting voices together, and seek agreement on shared purpose.
🌏

Syncretic Global Vision

Expanding the humanities to include Asian Knowledge Systems — building intellectual bridges between traditions rather than privileging any single one.

🔥

Lighting Fires, Not Filling Pails

Teaching and leading as a syncretic art — interactive, effortless, harmonious — where new knowledge is spontaneously created, not passively received.

🤝

People-First Leadership

Actively soliciting input, respecting differences, bringing conflicting voices together — because leaders fail when they stop taking people with them.

☮️

Scholarship for Peace

Convinced that long-term global peace depends on the objective, empathetic study of the world's diverse faiths, cultures, and knowledge traditions.
